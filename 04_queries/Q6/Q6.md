# Q6 – Analysis of Establishments That Do Not Show Improvement

## Analysis Question

What are the **common characteristics of establishments that do not show
improvement over time** between the first and the last inspection?

In particular, the analysis aims to identify:

- the **geographic distribution** of non-improving establishments
- the **most recurring violations**
- any **structural patterns** that may explain the lack of positive evolution

---

## Analytical Context

Q6 represents the **natural extension of Q5**.

In Q5 it emerged that:
- only a minority of establishments improve over the long term
- the majority maintain similar scores or worsen

Q6 therefore focuses on the **subset of non-improving establishments**
to better understand the underlying causes.

The data model used includes:

- **Fact table:** `inspection_events_table`
- **Dimensions:** `establishment_dim`, `area_dim`, `inspection_dim`

---

## Definition of “Non-improving Establishment”

An establishment is considered **non-improving** if:

```

last_score ≥ first_score

```

Recall that:
- a **higher score indicates worse conditions**
- therefore, a final score greater than or equal to the initial one implies
  the absence of structural improvement

---

## Analysis Logic

The analysis is structured in three steps:

1. **Identification of non-improving establishments**
   - comparison between first and last inspection (Q5 logic)

2. **Geographic distribution**
   - count of non-improving establishments by area

3. **Recurring violations**
   - identification of the most frequent violation codes associated with these establishments

This approach makes it possible to distinguish between:
- territorial factors
- operational and structural factors

## Q6a – Geographic Distribution of Non-improving Establishments

| Brooklyn       |                         3,900 |                5,003 |     77.96% |
| Queens         |                         3,343 |                4,313 |     77.49% |
| Bronx          |                         1,313 |                1,684 |     77.98% |
| Staten Island  |                           557 |                  721 | **77.26%** |

---

### Insight (Q6a)

- the percentage of non-improving establishments is **very similar across all areas**
- no borough emerges as a **clear outlier**
- the distribution reflects the overall density of establishments

This suggests that the lack of positive evolution is **not primarily a geographic phenomenon**.

---

## Q6 Conclusions

The analysis shows that non-improving establishments:

- are not concentrated in a single geographic area
- exhibit **repeated patterns of non-compliance**
- tend to address issues only temporarily

This indicates that:

- corrective actions are effective as an immediate response
- but do not always lead to lasting improvements
  in internal operating processes
